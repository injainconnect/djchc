Explore sacred Digambar Jain literature and scriptures

Digambar Jain Books Collection

Samayasāra

By Ācārya Kundakunda

A foundational text on the nature of the soul, exploring the distinction between the self and non-self from the Nishchay (absolute) viewpoint.

Pravachanasāra

By Ācārya Kundakunda

Essence of the doctrine — covers Jiva, Ajiva, and the path to liberation through right faith, knowledge, and conduct.

Tattvārtha Sūtra

By Ācārya Umāsvāti

The most authoritative Jain text accepted by all sects, systematically presenting the seven tattvas (realities) of Jain philosophy.

Ratnakaraṇḍa Śrāvakācāra

By Ācārya Samantabhadra

A guide to the conduct and duties of a Digambar Jain householder, covering vratas, anuvratas, and daily practices.

Ṣaṭkhaṇḍāgama

By Ācārya Pushpadanta & Bhūtabali

One of the oldest surviving Digambar Jain texts, a massive treatise on karma theory in six volumes.

Āptamīmāṁsā

By Ācārya Samantabhadra

A logical examination of the qualities of a Jina (omniscient being) and the validity of Jain teachings through rigorous reasoning.

Gommaṭasāra

By Ācārya Nemichandra

A comprehensive compendium covering Jīvakāṇḍa (soul) and Karmakāṇḍa (karma), essential for understanding Digambar metaphysics.

Pañcāstikāya

By Ācārya Kundakunda

Describes the five eternal substances — Jīva, Pudgala, Dharma, Adharma, and Ākāśa — and their nature and interactions.

Tiloyapaṇṇatti

By Ācārya Yativṛṣabha

A Prakrit text describing the cosmology of the three worlds (Triloka) according to Digambar Jain tradition.
